Current Research

  • Modelling Perceptions of Gerrymandering
    • I am currently working with Professor Lirong Xia and Inwon Kang on investigating how we can model and predict voter perceptions of gerrymandering. We hope to produce a model capable of correctly predicting how a given district assignment for a population will be perceived as either fair or unfair. Our hope is to gain insight into how citizens judge their districts and allow fair redistricting efforts to not just ensure objective fairness, but also that citizens will trust the assignment as well.
    • This work began in the fall of 2021 is currently ongoing.

Past Research

  • Gerrymandering under Uncertain Preferences
    • Begun as a project in Professor Lirong Xia's Economics and Computation class, this project investigated the problem of gerrymandering as a graph problem. Existing definitions of gerrymandering assumed voters have known, fixed preferences, which does not necessarily reflect the way people vote. How does the inclusion of probabilistic voter preferences affect the complexity of gerrymandering, and can we make and efficient algorithm to approximate solutions?
    • I looked into existing literature and identified the above research question, and worked on both the theoretical complexity increase caused by considering uncertain voter preferences. I also produced a greedy algorithm to approximate solutions to the problem, and experimentally showed its success and limitations.
    • After the class, I worked with Professor Xia to revise the original paper I wrote into a student abstract, which was submitted to the AAAI-2022 conference.
    • I worked on this project from the spring of 2021 to the fall of 2021, when the student abstract was accepted.
  • SCIENCE with Blockchain
    • The SCIENCE with Blockchain project is an investigation within RPI's IDEA Center on using blockchain and semantic web technologies to build a system capable of faciliating biomedical research data sharing agreements.
    • I joined at the start of the project and was responsible for investigating existing work on frameworks for semantic web techonologies and their intersection with blockchain technologies. I also was responsible for designing the SCIENCE ontology that would be the backbone of the system.
    • Finally, I worked to investigate how we can automatically convert ontologies into smart contracts, capable of fully representing the data sharing agreements commonly used in the biomedical research cycle.
    • I worked on this project during the spring semester of 2021.
  • HEALS Project
    • The HEALS project is a collaboration between RPI and IBM within RPI's IDEA Center on using semantic web technologies and autonomic reasoners to help physicians decide treatment plans for patients with type 2 diabetes.
    • I joined the project as a developer for a web interface that wolid display the output of the reasoner, both the decision and the reasoning for that decision.
    • My worked used standard web development tools like HTML, CSS, and Javascript, as well as ontological languages like RDF formatted data and SPARQL to query the RDF database.
    • I worked on this project during RPI's Arch summer semester in 2020.
